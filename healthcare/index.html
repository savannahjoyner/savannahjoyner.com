Seeking Healthcare Abroad

OVERVIEW

ROLE

This project was the outcome of a semester long project within the course User Interface Design. While creating this project, my team utilized formal interaction design processes to brainstorm, map, create, iterate upon and test our product.

This project was very research and process heavy in an effort to fully assess user needs and experience. Much of the semester was spent refining our goals and tweaking our designs and assumptions about the applications uses. This process allowed us to identify a need that has not been previously addressed by any technologies.

UX Researcher


User Research, User Testing


TOOLS USED

Figma, Miro, Indesign, Qualtrics

CHALLENGE

Study abroad students are generally unprepared to handle sudden medical emergencies. At the time of need, finding a local hospital, clinic, or pharmacy, especially for students with chronic and frequent health issues can be difficult. In most cases, extensive research is required but in the case of emergency there is little time for this research. Some pressure could be taken off student with a resource that consolidates information about local healthcare facilities, insurance info, medical history info, common diseases/symptoms, etc.

Discovery Phase


Task Analysis-Storyboarding

We created visual storyboards corresponding to 3 tasks performed by the user, that we felt was unsupported by technology.



Thinking About the Big Picture

At this stage we assessed existing systems, media, and research/literature available to learn about solutions that already exist in the world. From this research we found that any information or resources for students were spread across many sources and medium. There did not exist any app or website that focused helping users with this issue. Any prior knowledge on healthcare access prior to travelling would have to come from extensive individual research.



User Reasearch

We conducted a large qualtrics survey as well as individual interviews in order to fully empathize with and understand users.




Affinity Mapping

We reviewed all data as a team and participated in an affinity diagramming exercise. By categorizing these notes, we were able to identify challenges our users face which provided design implications. The affinity diagram provided visibility into trends among all key user groups.

Low Fidelity Prototypes

From our affinity mapping, we found 3 potential solutions for our application. We created low fidelity prototypes for each of these solutions in order to better predict and refine their uses.

One Stop Shop Healthcare

After looking back on our research we realized that the problems our respondents had while studying abroad were varied, ranging from finding medicine in their local area to trying to figure out how where the hospitals were. The one stop shop attempts to cover all bases by providing users a quick and easy way to locate general healthcare information in their area. Important information for clinics such as phone number and location can quickly be located. Symptoms and treatments of local diseases can be easily learned.

Medicine Finder

During our research, we found that many people had a common struggle when it came to finding medicine while in a foreign country. Some issues that kept users from successfully acquiring medicine included difficult navigation to a pharmacy, difference in types of medicine provided, and language barriers in medicine names. This app makes it easier for users to locate medicine and pharmacies based on their location and symptoms. It also gives common english equivalents so that the user can feel comfortable taking something they recognize.

Yelp for Healthcare

We concluded through our research that students who study abroad are generally very unprepared when it comes to dealing with health care and emergency issues abroad. Especially when abroad, it is even more difficult to find and locate health care services/providers that are best fit for you and closest in proximity. This ‘Yelp’ style app makes it easy for the user to simply input their current location and be provided with a list of facilities near them with the best reviews and ratings. The user can write their own review as well, describing their own experience so future users can also have access to this information.

Implementation and Evaluation


Solution

Through an in class feedback session, we were able to recieve tips and suggestions on each of our prototypes. After recieving feedback we found that 70% of students selected One Stop Shop as their prefered app. We decided to refine and expand our One Stop Shop application design for our final product.



"Within Team" Summative Evaluation

Upon the completion of our prototype we conducted a summative evalution in order to find any bugs. For our summative evaluation, we conducted a cognitive walkthrough. In order to do this we all joined a zoom call. The facilitator (Savannah) provided the other team members with a list of tasks they were to emulate while using the prototype. The overarching scenario was that of a study abroad student in need of some sort of medical/healthcare information. Several of the tasks involved the user being sick and needing to find either a clinic, hospital or pharmacy for medical attention. In other tasks, users were looking to plan ahead and find clinics in areas they would be travelling or update their information in case of emergency. More simple tasks consisted of basic app functionality such as “easily navigate the main menu” or “check and see if insurance information is up to date”.





Top 5 Changes:

  • Add a feature so that the user can save clinics/hospitals/pharmacies to their profile to view later. This would allow for the user to reduce the number of searches that they make for a clinic/hospital/pharmacy location.
  • Add a pharmacy navigation similar to hospitals and clinics. By adding in pharmacies, the user is able to access more health information that they might need.
  • Add more info on clinics (website, rating, etc). By adding in more clinic information, this gives users a wider range of options, and it allows for the user to not blindly choose a place to receive medical attention.
  • Rework main menu language and layout for user clarity (specifically info vs profile sections)
  • Add more info within the insurance section in case the user needs to access it. By adding in more information about insurance, this saves the user time in their searches. The user is able to find the information they need in one place.
