CFM is looking to communicate to vendors and customers how the market and the larger product lifecycle operate while better encouraging vendors to commit to certified organic food, locally grown produce, compostable packaging, and decreasing food miles.

Solution

We decided to create a resource to engage and educate vendors, consumers, and members of the community about sustainability. Our goal was to celebrate and award the vendors who have demonstrated sustainable practices based on the product life cycle.

With the WOZ approach, we were able to have our website evaluated from different vendor and consumer point of views (each participant acting as a different persona) while also curating the intended interactions as the wizard.

Reflection

My main regret with this project is shifting our design from a vendor portal to an educational website. We made this change after our client advised us against creating technology dependent on vendor interaction. In a professional environment, we likely would have had access to conversation with vendors and also a much larger time frame. In that case, upon receiving pushback from our client, I would have conducted user testing to truly assess the vendors' receptiveness to an interactive app. Because that was not a possibility within the confines of a semester-long course, we trusted our clients' knowledge of vendors and rerouted the project. Looking back I'm hesitant about whether we made the right change. The switch stifled interaction. In the end, we created not much more than an interactive PowerPoint.

This is one of those projects where I look at the final outcome and feel a bit disappointed. Nevertheless, I wanted it in my portfolio because I am proud of the research and design process that went into it. This was one of the first formal design processes I got to be a part of and I distinctly remember experiencing the feeling of 'wow, this is where I'm supposed to be'. So even though I don't claim this as my best work, I'm proud of what it taught me and continues to teach me about myself and what kind of designer I want to be.
