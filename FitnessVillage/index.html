Fitness Village

OVERVIEW

ROLE

This prototype was the outcome of a semester long project within the course Education Technology. While creating this project, my team utilized research and design processes to brainstorm, create, and test a learning interaction guided by our SWBAT(Students will be able to) learning goals. I led Product Design while my teammates handled Research and Testing.

Product Designer


UI Design, UX Design, Prototyping, User Testing


TOOLS USED

Figma, Miro, Microsoft Teams

CHALLENGE

We were to create an learning app that met the following requirements. Our team was focused on keeping children physically active, while their lives had suddenly required most of their time to be spent inside on their devices.

Discovery Phase

Reviewing Literature and Existing Technology

Download Literature Review



We conducted a literature review of relevant articles as well as comparable educational technologies. Through this review, we strengthened our understanding of the learning environment, the target learners, and the ways to promote transfer. We synthesized several positive elements to include and negative elements to avoid in our own educational technology.



Top Takeaways:

  • We will strive to create a platform that is customizable. Allowing creativity makes the platform more enjoyable, and therefore, make the user more inclined to use it.
  • Our app will include feedback. Feedback is defined as any “informative or evaluative” description of the user’s behavior, and it has been shown to increase user self-efficacy, motivation, and learning.
  • Integration of parent and children's interactions into one platform could create more effective learning as children will need support outside of technology to maintain healthy lifestyles.
  • Users may feel more supported and encouraged if they are able to engage in a broader community or keep up with other children.

Our Solution

In 2020, Covid-19 is a global pandemic that has uprooted and rewritten most parts of our lives. Our team is looking to offer an alternative solution to fill the gap in children’s physical education. Within this project, we will design an app that addresses the issue of online learning and hopefully provide interesting and engaging ways to combat sedentary behavior in children even after schools return to normal. In this context, we seek to create an online platform to enable children to stay physically and mentally healthy. This will take the form of a phone app geared toward elementary school children in grades two through five. The app will create an immersive, virtual community, modeled after a small village or environment, in which AIs and potentially real-life users of the app are represented visually as customizable human avatars. Upon opening the app, the user will enter their age, height, and weight and customize their own avatar. They can then explore the village community and chat with the NPCs by tapping around the screen to move their avatar. While completing the activities, the phone’s front-facing camera activates in order to record the child’s movements, and the avatar onscreen reflects the child’s motions.



SWBAT Goals

Students will be able to...

  • Describe the importance of physical activity in creating healthy practices
  • Accurately move their body according to instruction
  • Demonstrate spatial awareness
  • Describe the importance of emotional health in creating healthy practices
  • Regulate their emotional state more easily

Prototyping and Peer Feedback


We used Figma for our prototyping. After completing our initial version of our prototype we created a video in order to get feedback from our peers.



Watch Prototype Video:

Feedback Driven Improvements:

  • Delete sign-up/log-in procedure as it may be too complicated for young children and not necessarily required for our purposes. Now users can simply download the app, create their avatar, and start playing.
  • Create additional screen before the game warning the user to clear the space around them in order to move around adequately during the activities. This will ensure proper use and safety.
  • Add interactive villagers to the village map. Players can interact with these villagers by talking to them, which would prompt the child to pick an activity they haven’t in a while, or by gifting them an item in their inventory, which would change their appearance on the map. This adds additional incentivization to earning gold coins.

Reflection

This project was really fun to create. As I am usually the UX designer rather than a graphic designer in most of my projects, it was nice to flex that muscle and I am proud of the results. The graphics of the avatar and the idea of navigating a village are the strongest points of our design. The idea of immersion in “another world” was a source of excitement to all of our participants.

If this app were to be built, the biggest challenge we may face is coming up with enough different activities to keep students engaged while exercising. After all, there are only so many different real-life scenarios in which legitimate exercise movements, such as a squat or lunge, might occur. Our other weakness is in the actual mechanics of seeing the user’s movements — it is unclear whether a phone’s frontfacing camera would be sufficient or if a wearable device may be necessary, which would unfortunately create a barrier to entry and limit our audience.

Overall, I enjoyed creating a concept that could be helpful in the midst of the pandemic. This course was entirely online, so as we created, we often felt the pains of this sedentary work. Our passions for fitness allowed us to create this innovative concept in hopes of helping children live more active lifestyles
